Peptilogics 2022

Topics:Peptide Folding, PyRosetta, Rosetta, Ab Initio Folding

During the summer going into my junior year, I had the privilege of working at Peptilogics, a company focused on peptide-based theraputics.
  • The first project was to take in custom information encoded on a PDB file, and display it in the log of a molecular visualization program called ChimeraX. The code was written in python using the chimerax.core.commands library, and enabled simulation scientists to have a cleaner view of their desired data from an ensemble PDB.
  • The second project also involved adding commands to ChimeraX. My task was to create a command that allows scientists to color PDB residues based on custom attributes besides b-factors like Rosetta Energy Score.
  • The third task was to create a peptide folding pipeline for the company using the ab initio script on Rosetta. I learned how to use the pyRosetta python library, Rosetta suite, python, and bash scripting to make pipelines to fold peptides in batch. To measure our performance, we used the RMSD and pandas, numpy toolkits to compare against industry standards Alphafold and PEPFOLD. My custom parameters and procedure was able to outperform both programs in the use case of peptides, and now is now being used by the company in their drug discovery pipeline.

Tartan Hacks 2023

Title: Anwei

Awards: Design for America (DFA) Prize for Best Social Impact Project

Tartan Hacks is the biggest hackathon in Pittsburgh hosted by CMU each year. I wanted my project to address the steep price of therapy, so I designed a prototype AI therapist powered by an OpenAI model I fine tuned myself on therapist transcripts.

The final product was a seamless website that allowed users to:

  • Fill out exercises to help them process their thoughts, anxieties, and worries
  • Chat with a therapist as if they were texting them
  • Sometimes, we need a face to face interaction though, so I made a prototype animated therapist that you can speak to as if it was a video call

I was working by myself for this hackathon, so I was pretty thrilled when my project won the best social impact prize :)

This project was a great exercise to bolster my full stack development skills. In addition it was the first time I worked with the OpenAI api, text-to-speech packages, and also my first time putting my random animation knowledge to use.

Fun fact: Anwei in Chinese, means "to comfort".

Tartan Hacks 2022

Title: Piano Translator

Awards: Finalist in Overall Competition, Third Place

Tartan Hacks is the biggest hackathon in Pittsburgh hosted by CMU each year. During this time, I was starting to get back into piano practice, but all of the songs I wanted to play did not have sheet music. However they did have synthesia videos, which is just a video of a keyboard at the bottom with notes falling down the screen DDR style/Tap-tap revolution style. My teammate and I sought to turn these videos into sheet music music computer vision.

  • This project leveraged full stack development skills to provide a platform which users can use the tool.
  • It also required a lot of algorithmic creativity as we had to think of ways to detect notes on a keyboard, what they were, when they were played, interpret musical rhythms from the number of seconds a note was played. Not to mention we had to figure out how to separate left and right hand notes, and piece them together.
  • Finally, we had to take all that information and translate into Music XML format which is how we decided to encode the sheet music information.

We presented this at the final round of TartanHacks 2022, and you can see our demo at the timestamp 51:36. Please note I was indeed extremely sleep deprived during the presentation, so my coherent speaking capability was somewhat hindered:
